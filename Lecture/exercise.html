About the City

Addis Ababa is the political and administrative heart of Ethiopia, often referred to as the political capital of Africa due to its diplomatic significance. Founded in 1886 by Emperor Menelik II and Empress Taytu Betul, the city emerged as a royal settlement around natural hot springs and quickly grew into a center of governance, culture, and modernization.

Main Attractions

Nestled in a highland plateau, it blends historical depth with rapid urban development and serves as a key hub for continental institutions like the African Union. The city reflects a dynamic mix of tradition and progress, with a vibrant cultural life and deep historical layers stretching back to early human history.
